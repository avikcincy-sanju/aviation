Position data comes from OpenSky Network's free volunteer receiver network, so coverage is best over the US, Europe, and other well-covered regions — a quiet scope can just mean no nearby receiver is reporting, not necessarily an empty sky. Temporary OpenSky or browser-network interruptions are retried automatically with progressively longer pauses, while the most recent successful aircraft data remains visible. Aircraft type, registration, and registered owner come from hexdb.io when available. Scheduled airline origin and destination come from adsbdb.com by matching the callsign; airport names, cities, countries, coordinates, and elevations are shown when supplied. Route progress and time remaining are straight-line estimates, not official operational ETAs. When no scheduled route exists—common for private/general-aviation tail numbers—the page loads the public OurAirports dataset and shows the nearest known airport, clearly labeled as proximity only and not a confirmed origin or destination. "Registered in" is the aircraft's registration country, not its departure airport. Squawk 7500/7600/7700 are universal emergency transponder codes, and "ident activated" reflects a pilot pressing the ident button at ATC's request—both shown for awareness, not diagnosis.
