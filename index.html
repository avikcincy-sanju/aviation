Position data comes from OpenSky Network's free volunteer receiver network, so coverage is best over the US, Europe, and other well-covered regions — a quiet scope can just mean no nearby receiver is reporting, not necessarily an empty sky. Each card also shows GPS (geometric) altitude alongside barometric altitude, how the position was derived (ADS-B, multilateration, etc.), and how many seconds old the last update is, since ground-based coverage can lag a little behind an aircraft's real position. Aircraft type, registration, and registered owner come from hexdb.io's FAA-derived registry when available. Scheduled origin and destination come from adsbdb.com by matching the OpenSky callsign. Route data is crowd-sourced and may be unavailable, incomplete, or outdated; private/general-aviation tail-number callsigns usually have no scheduled route. "Registered in" is the aircraft's country of registration, not its flight's origin airport. Squawk 7500/7600/7700 are real universal emergency transponder codes (hijack / radio failure / general emergency), and "ident activated" reflects a pilot pressing the ident button at ATC's request — both shown here for awareness, not diagnosis.
