Position data comes from OpenSky Network's free volunteer receiver network, so coverage is best over the US, Europe, and other well-covered regions — a quiet scope can just mean no nearby receiver is reporting, not necessarily an empty sky. Aircraft type/registration comes from hexdb.io when available. "Registered in" is the aircraft's country of registration, not its flight's origin airport — full departure/arrival route data needs a paid API (e.g. FlightAware AeroAPI), since the free route APIs that exist don't support HTTPS and can't be called safely from a page hosted on GitHub Pages. Squawk 7500/7600/7700 are real universal emergency transponder codes (hijack / radio failure / general emergency) shown here for awareness, not diagnosis.
